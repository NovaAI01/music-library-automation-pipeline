## Reply if they ask what data is needed

A CSV, spreadsheet, XML, JSON, or catalog export is enough to start.

Useful fields are artist, title, release title, version text, identifiers,
label, release date or year, track number, disc number, duration, and any
internal catalog IDs. It is fine if some of those fields are missing; I can
confirm what is usable after seeing the export structure.

## Reply if they ask whether audio is needed

No. The audit only needs metadata.

You do not need to provide audio files, and the report does not rely on audio
analysis.

## Reply if they ask price

For the pilot, I am testing free or discounted audits in exchange for direct
feedback.

The pricing hypothesis after that is £49-£99 for a small catalog audit and
£149-£299 for a larger one. Those ranges are not fixed pricing yet; part of the
pilot is validating whether the report is valuable enough to pay for.

## Reply if they are worried about privacy

That is reasonable. The pilot can use a limited export with only the fields
needed for metadata QA, and it does not require audio files or access to your
source system.

The report is based on the metadata you choose to provide. I can also work from
a sample export first so you can judge whether the findings are useful before
sharing a larger catalog file.
